My father is Bill Spooner — guitarist, vocalist, co-founder, and principal songwriter of The Tubes. I designed and built his website to give him a home on the internet worthy of a fifty-year career in rock and roll.

The Tubes

The Tubes formed in San Francisco in 1972 from two Phoenix bands that had migrated west — Bill's group the Beans, and the Red, White and Blues Band. The result was one of the most theatrical and unpredictable acts in rock: elaborate stage productions, satirical characters, and music that defied easy categorization. Bill was the band's guitarist, vocalist, and one of its primary songwriters for nearly two decades.

He co-wrote White Punks on Dope — the sardonic 1975 debut single that became the band's signature — alongside Roger Steen. Later came Talk to Ya Later, Mondo Bondage, What Do You Want from Life?, and the 1983 top-10 hit She's a Beauty, whose video ran in heavy rotation in the early days of MTV. Albums were recorded with Todd Rundgren producing. The band toured relentlessly and built a devoted following on both sides of the Atlantic.

Solo & Post-Tubes

After leaving the Tubes in 1989, Bill eventually returned to writing and recording on his own terms. He released First Chud in 1985, Mall to Mars in 1997, and the Folk-Ups — an acoustic rock-folk group — became a fixture at Bay Area venues including Slim's and the Sweetwater, opening for Dave Davies of the Kinks among others.

The Site

billspooner.com is a clean, music-forward site housing his biography, discography, and catalog — built to reflect the same directness that characterizes his songwriting. The design strips away noise and lets the work speak. Like the man himself: no fuss, just fifty years of very good songs.
