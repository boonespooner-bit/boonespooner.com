Google Now Cards

product · ui · ux

When Google Now was first introduced (RIP), they approached us about an early access integration.

I worked directly with their product team to develop a series of timely, habit based cards that would help our core users quickly and easily get to their content.



New Content Available: We would notify a user that they could listen to new content available from a radio show they had favorited, as soon as it became available - or was about to go live on TuneIn.



New Episodes Available: when a new podcast episode became available for a favorite piece of content that the user followed, we would notify them and deeplink directly into the play experience.



Recommended Stations: I proposed building Recommended Stations - a simple list of recommended stations based off of your listening history to encourage discovery. This card could take on many shapes or formats depending on how often it, or the TuneIn app, was used by the user.



"Near You": I also proposed building out a location based card which would prompt a traveling user to explore radio near their location. Google Now often promoted items "new you" and this feature would help both TuneIn discovery as well as user discovery.
