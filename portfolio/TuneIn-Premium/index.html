TuneIn Premium

product · subscription · audio

As Senior Director of Product at TuneIn, I led the launch of TuneIn Premium — the company's first paid subscription tier and its most ambitious product bet. We set out to build the most comprehensive audio subscription service in the world, bundling live sports, audiobooks, ad-free music, and language learning into a single $7.99/month offering. The challenge was significant: TuneIn had built its audience on free radio. Converting listeners into paying subscribers required a value proposition that went well beyond ad removal — we needed content that couldn't be found anywhere else. What We Launched TuneIn Premium launched on August 25, 2015, in the US, UK, and Canada with four core pillars: Live play-by-play coverage of every Major League Baseball game and every Barclays Premier League and Bundesliga match. For sports fans, this meant every game, every team, every broadcast — home and away feeds included. Unlimited access to more than 40,000 audiobook titles from publishers including Penguin Random House, HarperCollins, and Scholastic. Over 600 music stations listened to commercial-free — a direct answer to satellite radio. Sixteen language learning programs, rounding out a catalog that stretched well beyond anything audio had bundled before. The Bigger Picture TuneIn Premium was positioned as a challenger to satellite radio — a modern, on-demand alternative that lived in your pocket and worked across every device. The NFL partnership followed just weeks later in October 2015, adding the #1 domestic sports league to the Premium offering and cementing TuneIn's position as the home for live audio sports.
