TuneIn 2014 Launch

product · ux

In 2014 TuneIn underwent a massive redesign and relaunch. It was a months-long company wide effort to ship a completely new experience to millions of users.

Below is an overview of some of the pieces that I created, planned and product managed for the release. These were all accompanied by detail spec that lived in our shared wiki space "Confluence."

Mobile Flow Mapping
I created several critical mobile mapping flows to help our engineering team understand how we expected parts of the app to behave. These "mapping" docs became the de-facto requirements for our launch.

New User Mobile Paths Wireframes Android New User Mobile Paths Glossy Layout iOS New User Mobile Paths Glossy Layout OnBoarding
For the first time we introduced an onboarding flow with TuneIn. This flow was designed to get you to content we thought you'd love based on your location, the most popular content near you, and an editorial selection of the best content we had available.

Mobile Glossy Mocks Web Glossy Mocks Browse
As part of this launch we also redesigned our main Browse experience. We introduced the notion of "galleries" which were a horizontally organized list of boxes that a user could swipe through.

I started with wireframes and then worked with the design team to create glossy mocks for the engineering team.

Mobile Wireframes Mobile Glossy Mocks Web Glossy Mocks Explore
We also introduced the notion of the Explore page. We created this and ran it on 20% of users to understand how it would help them improve discovery of new content.
